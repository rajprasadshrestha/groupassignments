The employment outlook will be good for Legislators (NOC 00010) in Northwest Territories for the 2023-2025 period.

The following factors contributed to this outlook:

  • Employment growth will lead to a few new positions.
  • Several positions will become available due to retirements.
  • There are a small number of unemployed workers with recent experience in this occupation.

Here are some key facts about Legislators in Northwest Territories:

  • Approximately 50 people worked in this occupation in May 2021.
  • Legislators mainly work in the following sectors:
    • Public Administration and Defence (NAICS 91): 85%
  • The distribution of full-time and part-time workers in this occupation is:
    • Full-time workers: 87% compared to 88% for all occupations
    • Part-time workers: 13% compared to 12% for all occupations
  • The gender distribution of people in this occupation is:
    • Men: 53% compared to 52% for all occupations
    • Women: 47% compared to 48% for all occupations
  • The educational attainment of workers in this occupation is:
    • No high school diploma: 12% compared to 17% for all occupations
    • High school diploma or equivalent: 12% compared to 26% for all occupations
    • Apprenticeship or trades certificate or diploma: 24% compared to 12% for all occupations
    • College certificate or diploma or university certificate below bachelor's: 24% compared to 18% for all occupations
    • Bachelor's degree: 18% compared to 17% for all occupations
    • University certificate, degree or diploma above bachelor level: 12% compared to 9% for all occupations

The employment outlook will be moderate for Human resources managers (NOC 10011) in the Saskatoon - Biggar region for the 2023-2025 period.

    • Professional, scientific and technical services (NAICS 54): 17%

    • Mining and quarrying (NAICS 212): 8%
    • Finance, insurance and Real estate and rental and leasing (NAICS 52-53): 8%
